## Text Classification

The Chaldean Oracles (2nd century CE, attributed to Julian the Chaldean or Julian the Theurgist) are a collection of hexameter verse fragments preserved through quotation in later Neoplatonists (Proclus, Porphyry, Iamblichus, Psellus, Pletho). They are not a single text but ~38 oracle fragments in this collection, with fuller versions elsewhere.

The text file contains:
- Preface (Sapere Aude) — editorial apparatus
- Introduction by L.O. — includes a structural diagram of the Chaldean cosmological scheme
- 38 numbered oracle fragments with attributions and brief commentary

**For encoding purposes:** The Introduction's structural diagram (the "CHALDAEAN SCHEME") is the single most useful resource — it lays out the cosmological levels explicitly. The oracle fragments then provide the textual warrant for specific nodes and relationships.

## Key Observations

1. **Double-demiurge structure:** The most distinctive Chaldean feature. The Second Mind creates the intelligible/empyrean cosmos; Hypezokos (Flower of Fire) builds the material world. No other tradition in the corpus has this two-stage demiurgic architecture.

2. **Linear chain topology:** The Chaldean schema is linear (depth 5, max branching = 1) — second linear chain after Plotinus. But the functional roles are very different: Hecate (soul) at level 1 vs. Nous (intellect) at Plotinus level 1.

3. **Hecate as World-Soul:** Hecate occupies the position that Plotinus assigns to Psyche/Soul — she is the mediating principle between the transcendent Father and the creating Mind. But she is explicitly named, mythologized, and theurgically active in ways Plotinian Soul is not.

4. **The triadic law:** "In every World shineth the Triad, over which the Monad ruleth." (Oracle 36) The Chaldean system is explicitly organized around triads at every level — this structural self-similarity may be relevant to the topology analysis.

5. **Fragmentary source problem:** Unlike the Enneads (systematic treatises) or the Apocryphon of John (complete narrative), the Chaldean Oracles are fragments. The schema is reconstructed from these fragments + the Introduction's structural diagram. This introduces more interpretive uncertainty than the other traditions.
