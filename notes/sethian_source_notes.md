## Text Classification
Trimorphic Protennoia is a Sethian Gnostic text, roughly contemporaneous with the Apocryphon of John (mid-2nd century CE). It belongs to the Barbeloite branch of Sethian Gnosticism, characterized by the central role of Barbelo (here = Protennoia) and the framework of the Four Luminaries, Sophia's fall, and Yaltabaoth's creation.

The tradition slot in the WP 1.1 roadmap is labeled "Sethian Gnostic (Tradition 2)" — this text directly represents that tradition, unlike the Apocryphon which was labeled "Valentinian" in the roadmap for historical reasons (see encoding_decisions Entry 10).

## Key Observations

1. **The Sethian Core is Stable:** Both the Apocryphon of John and the Trimorphic Protennoia use the same cosmological framework (Invisible Spirit → Barbelo → Autogenes → Four Luminaries → Sophia → Yaltabaoth → Material World). This confirms the existence of a shared Sethian school of cosmological thought. Turner (2001) documents this shared framework extensively.

2. **Protennoia's Unique Topology:** The triadic first emanation (Voice/Speech/Word) is the TP's most distinctive structural feature. It creates a semantically richer first_emanation node than any other tradition. For the topology analysis, this won't differentiate the graph structure but will appear in the semantic comparison.

3. **Sophia as Victim:** The shift from Sophia-as-agent-of-error (Apocryphon) to Sophia-as-innocent-victim (TP) is a significant semantic variation within the shared topology. Both use the `fallen` role and `fragmentation` edges, but the moral valence is inverted. This is an important finding for the paper's discussion of intra-school variation.

4. **The Eleleth Trigger:** In TP, Yaltabaoth appears when Eleleth (the fourth Luminary) proclaims "I am King!" — the creation of the lower cosmos is triggered by a LUMINOUS proclamation, not by Sophia's direct unauthorized act. This is different mechanism-wise but same topology-wise.

5. **Confirmation of Cross-Traditional Findings:** The near-identity of the Apocryphon and TP schemas confirms that the Gnostic "deep tree with fragmentation pivot" topology is a consistent Sethian signature, not idiosyncratic to one text.
