# Plotinus Enneads — Key Passages for Emanation-Schema Encoding

**Source:** Plotinus, *The Enneads*, trans. Stephen MacKenna, rev. B.S. Page (London: Faber, 1969)
**PDF:** `Research/plotinustheenneaMackenna,Stephen.pdf` (702 pages)

Only passages directly relevant to the emanation hierarchy are excerpted here. Page numbers refer to the PDF.

---

## 1. The One (to Hen) — The Absolute Source

### V.1.1-3 (PDF pp. 429-431): The One as Origin

> "What can it be that has brought the souls to forget the father, God, and, though members of the Divine and entirely of that world, to ignore at once themselves and It?"

> "The evil that has overtaken them has its source in self-will, in the entry into the sphere of process, and in the primal differentiation with the desire for self-ownership."

> "Soul is the author of all living things, that it has breathed the life into them all... it is the maker of the sun; itself formed and ordered this vast heaven and conducts all that rhythmic motion: and it is a principle distinct from all these..."

> "The Soul once seen to be thus precious, thus divine, you may hold the faith that by its possession you are already nearing God: in the strength of this power make upwards towards Him: at no great distance you must attain: there is not much between."

### V.2.1 (PDF p. 440): The One as Generator of Being

> "The One is all things and no one of them; the source of all things is not all things; and yet it is all things in a transcendental sense — all things, so to speak, having run back to it."

> "It is precisely because there is nothing within the One that all things are from it: in order that Being may be brought about, the source must be no Being but Being's generator, in what is to be thought of as the primal act of generation."

### III.8.9-10 (PDF pp. 307-310): The One as Spring

> "Clearly a Being of this nature is not the primal existent; there must exist that which transcends it... In the first place, Plurality is later than Unity. The Intellectual-Principle is a number; and number derives from unity."

> "Imagine a spring that has no source outside itself; it gives itself to all the rivers, yet is never exhausted by what they take, but remains always integrally as it was."

> "Once you have uttered 'The Good', add no further thought: by any addition, and in proportion to that addition, you introduce a deficiency."

### VI.9.2-6 (PDF pp. 675-680): The One Beyond Being

> "The Intellectual-Principle cannot be the First. Intellect must be about the Intellectual Act: at least in its higher phase... it must be intent upon its Prior; its introversion is a conversion upon the Principle."

> "Any manifold, anything beneath The Unity, is dependent: combined from various constituents, its essential nature goes in need of unity; but unity cannot need itself; it stands unity accomplished."

**Summary for encoding:** The One is absolutely simple, beyond being and thought, the source of all by overflow. It is not "a being" but the generator of Being. It does not think or act — it simply overflows. This is the root node: `functional_role: source`.

## 2. Nous (Intellect / Divine Mind) — First Emanation

### V.1.4-7 (PDF pp. 432-436): Nous Emerges from the One

> "Let us mount to its archetype, to the yet more authentic sphere: there we are to contemplate all things as members of the Intellectual — eternal in their own right, vested with a self-springing consciousness and life — and, presiding over all these, the unsoiled Intelligence and the unapproachable wisdom."

> "That archetypal world is the true Golden Age, age of Kronos... For here is contained all that is immortal: nothing here but is Divine Mind; all is God; this is the place of every soul."

> "As a manifold, then, this God, the Intellectual-Principle, exists above the Soul... Bringing itself close to the divine..."

> "Everything moving has necessarily an object towards which it advances; but since the Supreme can have no such object, we may not ascribe motion to it: anything that comes into being after it can only be understood as generated while the One remains turned towards itself."

### V.1.7 (PDF p. 435): Nous as Image of the One

> "The Intellectual-Principle stands as the image of The One, firstly because there is a certain necessity that the first activity of anything should be a representation of that thing."

> "The offspring must seek and love the begetter; and especially so when begetter and begotten are alone in their sphere."

### V.1.8-9 (PDF pp. 437-438): Nous Contains the Forms

> "Knowing and Being are one thing... this unity is to him motionless in spite of the intellection he attributes to it."

> Nous is "pregnant with this offspring; for it has, so to speak, drawn all within itself again, holding them lest they fall away towards Matter."

**Summary for encoding:** Nous is the first emanation from the One. It arises by the One's overflow, then turns back to contemplate its source, and in this turning-and-contemplating becomes Intellect. It contains all the Forms (Ideas). Thinking and Being are identical at this level. `functional_role: first_emanation`.

## 3. Psyche (Soul) — The Third Hypostasis

### V.1.2-3 (PDF pp. 430-431): Soul as Maker of All Things

> "Soul is the author of all living things... it is the maker of the sun; itself formed and ordered this vast heaven and conducts all that rhythmic motion."

> "It is a principle distinct from all these to which it gives law and movement and life."

### V.1.3-5 (PDF pp. 431-433): Soul Below Nous

> "The Soul once seen to be thus precious, thus divine, you may hold the faith that by its possession you are already nearing God."

> "But over this... there exists a further principle, its source and archetype — the Intellectual-Principle [Nous]."

> "This God, the Intellectual-Principle, exists above the Soul here, the Soul which once for all stands linked a member of the divine, unless by a deliberate apostasy."

### V.2.1 (PDF p. 441): Soul as Further Outgoing

> "From the first principle to ultimate an outgoing in which unfailingly each principle retains its own seat while its offspring takes another rank, a lower, though on the other hand every being is in identity with its prior as long as it holds that contact."

### III.8.3-6 (PDF pp. 301-306): Soul and Contemplation

> "The reasoning-principle in the Soul acts upon the representations standing before it as the result of sense-perception."

> "All act aims at this knowing; all impulse is towards knowledge."

**Summary for encoding:** Soul (Psyche) is the third hypostasis, emanated from Nous. It is the animating principle of the cosmos — it makes, orders, and gives life to everything. It faces two ways: upward toward Nous and downward toward Nature and the material world. `functional_role: soul`.

## 4. Nature (Physis) — Soul's Lowest Expression

### III.8.1-4 (PDF pp. 299-303): Nature as Contemplation's Lowest Level

> "Supposing we played a little before entering upon our serious concern and maintained that all things are striving after Contemplation, looking to Vision as their one end..."

> "Nature... It derives from a Contemplation and some contemplating Being."

> "The Contemplation springing from the reasoning faculty — that, I mean, of planning its own content — it does not possess."

### V.2.1 (PDF p. 441): Nature in the Emanation Chain

> "In the case of soul entering some vegetal form, what is there is one phase, the more rebellious and less intellectual, outgone to that extreme."

**Summary for encoding:** Nature (Physis) is Soul's lowest productive activity — it produces the physical world through a kind of unconscious contemplation. It is not a separate hypostasis in the strict sense but a phase of Soul directed downward. This creates an encoding decision (see encoding_decisions.md). `functional_role: intermediary`.

## 5. Matter (Hyle) — The Lowest Level

### V.1.1 (PDF p. 429): Matter as Endpoint of Descent

> "They were hurried down the wrong path, and in the end, drifting further and further, they came to lose even the thought of their origin in the Divine."

### III.8.7 (PDF p. 305-306): Matter as Privation

> "What exists in an outside element is some mode of living-thing; it is not the Self-Living."

### VI.9.1 (PDF p. 675): Matter as Maximum Distance from Unity

> "Aggregates such as a choir are furthest from unity, the more compact are the nearer."

**Summary for encoding:** Matter (Hyle) is the endpoint of the emanation cascade — maximum distance from the One, minimum reality, almost pure privation. Plotinus debates whether it has any positive existence at all. `functional_role: matter`.

## 6. The Emanation Mechanism

### V.2.1 (PDF p. 440): The Overflow Principle

> "It is precisely because there is nothing within the One that all things are from it."

### III.8.10 (PDF pp. 309-310): The Spring Metaphor

> "Imagine a spring that has no source outside itself; it gives itself to all the rivers, yet is never exhausted by what they take."

### V.1.6 (PDF pp. 434-435): Two-Phase Emanation

The emanation follows a two-phase process:
1. **Overflow:** The higher principle produces a lower one by natural radiation/overflow
2. **Reversion:** The lower principle turns back to contemplate its source, and this contemplation constitutes its being

This two-phase structure (procession + reversion) is characteristic of the Plotinian system and distinguishes it from other traditions' emanation models.
