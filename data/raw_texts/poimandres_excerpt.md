# Corpus Hermeticum I: Poimandres — Key Passages for Schema Encoding

**Source:** Corpus Hermeticum Book I (Poimandres / Poemandres / The Shepherd of Men)
**Translation:** G.R.S. Mead, *Thrice-Greatest Hermes* (1906, public domain)
**Cross-reference:** Brian P. Copenhaver, *Hermetica* (Cambridge, 1992) for scholarly validation
**Digital source:** Internet Archive (archive.org) — full Mead translation

The Poimandres is a first-person revelation dialogue. Hermes Trismegistus recounts a vision in which Poimandres (identified as "Mind of all-masterhood" / Nous) reveals the origin of the cosmos and humanity.

---

## Section-by-Section Cosmogony (Sections 1-19)

### Sections 1-3: The Vision Opens
Hermes enters a meditative state. A vast Being appears and identifies itself as Poimandres, "the Mind of the Sovereignty" (Nous). Poimandres offers to teach Hermes about "the things that are, and understand their nature, and get knowledge of God."

**Schema relevance:** Establishes Poimandres = Nous = the Divine Mind as the revelatory authority.

### Section 4: The Primal Light
The vision begins with boundless Light — "a joyous Light" — which is identified as God, the Supreme Father. Then a Darkness appears, "fearful and gloomy," which transforms into a watery, turbulent Nature.

**Schema relevance:** Two primordial principles revealed:
- **Light** = God the Father = the Supreme Source (node: source)
- **Darkness/Moist Nature** = the primal material substrate (becomes Nature/Physis)

### Section 5: The Holy Word (Logos)
From the Light, a Holy Word (Logos) descends upon Nature. Fire leaps upward from the watery substance, and Air follows Fire. Earth and Water remain below, intermingled. The Logos moves over/upon the moist Nature.

**Schema relevance:** The **Logos** (Holy Word) is a distinct emanation from the Light, which acts upon Nature. This is NOT the same as Nous — it is produced by Nous/God and directed downward.

### Sections 6-7: Nous Produces the Demiurge
Poimandres explains: "That Light is I, thy God, Mind (Nous), prior to Moist Nature which appeared from Darkness." The Light-Word (Logos) is "the Son of God." Then: God-Mind (being male-female, as Life and Light) gave birth to another Mind — a Formative Mind (Demiurgos) — who, being God of Fire and Air, formed Seven Rulers (Governors) that encompass the sensible world in circles. Their governance is called Fate (Heimarmene).

**Schema relevance:** Critical branching point:
- **God-Mind** (the Supreme, Light-Life) produces:
  - **Logos** (the Word/Son — directed at Nature)
  - **Demiurgos** (Formative Mind — who creates the planetary architecture)
- **Demiurgos** produces:
  - **Seven Governors** (planetary powers governing Fate)

### Sections 8-11: The Cosmos Takes Shape
The Logos leaps up from the downward-tending elements and reunites with the Formative Mind (they are "of the same substance"). The irrational elements (earth, water) are left behind, governed by the Logos from above. Nature produces living creatures — animals without reason.

**Schema relevance:** The physical cosmos is now structured:
- Upper: Fire + Air (governed by the Seven Governors)
- Lower: Earth + Water (irrational nature, producing animals)

### Sections 12-14: Anthropos — The Primal Man
God the Father (Mind of all things, being Life and Light) gives birth to Man (Anthropos), "co-equal to Himself, and fell in love with him as His own child." Anthropos is beautiful, bearing the Father's image. God gave Man authority over all created things.

Anthropos gazes down through the Harmony (the planetary spheres) and shows his beautiful form to Nature below. **Nature sees Anthropos reflected in the water and his shadow upon the earth, and falls in love with him.** Anthropos, seeing his own form reflected in Nature, falls in love with that reflection and desires to dwell in it. He breaks through the Harmony and enters Nature.

**Schema relevance:** Anthropos is a DIRECT emanation from God (not from Demiurgos or Logos). He is:
- Produced at the same level as Demiurgos (both are "children" of God-Mind)
- His FALL into Nature is a fragmentation event (not emanation)

### Sections 15-17: The Union and Its Consequences
Nature embraces Anthropos, and they become one. This is why humanity is twofold: "mortal because of body, but because of the essential Man, immortal." The seven human births correspond to the seven Governors' natures.

**Schema relevance:** The material human being arises from the union of Anthropos + Nature. This is the endpoint of the descent.

### Sections 18-19: The Divine Proclamation
God commands: "Increase in increasing and multiply in multitude, all ye that have been made and crafted." Self-knowledge leads to deathlessness; ignorance leads to death.

**Schema relevance:** The return/ascent path is mentioned but NOT encoded in the primary descent graph per schema_spec.md rules.

## Key Structural Observations

1. **BRANCHING:** Unlike Plotinus, the Hermetic schema branches. God produces both Nous/Demiurgos (the cosmic-creating line) AND Anthropos (the human-creating line) as parallel emanations.

2. **THE LOGOS IS DISTINCT FROM NOUS:** In Plotinus, Logos is an activity of Nous. In Poimandres, Logos is a separate entity — the "Son of God" — that acts upon Nature independently.

3. **THE DEMIURGE IS NOT EVIL:** Unlike Gnostic systems where the Demiurge is ignorant or malicious, the Hermetic Demiurgos is a positive creative agent, produced by God-Mind for the purpose of ordering the cosmos.

4. **ANTHROPOS FALLS BY CHOICE:** The Primal Man chooses to descend into Nature out of love for his own reflection. This is fragmentation by desire, not by error (as in Sophia's fall in Gnosticism).

5. **NATURE IS PRE-EXISTING:** Moist Nature/Darkness appears to exist prior to (or alongside) the Light, not as something emanated from it. This is a cosmological dualism absent from Plotinus.
