## Sepher Yetzirah — Key Passages

**On the Ten Sephiroth:**
> "Ten are the ineffable Sephiroth. Twenty-two are the Letters, the Foundation of all things; there are Three Mothers, Seven Double and Twelve Simple letters." (Chapter I, §2)

> "Ten is the number of the ineffable Sephiroth, ten and not nine, ten and not eleven. Understand this wisdom, and be wise by the perception." (Chapter I, §4)

> "The Ten ineffable Sephiroth have ten vast regions bound unto them; boundless in origin and having no ending; an abyss of good and of ill; measureless height and depth..." (Chapter I, §5)

> "The Ten ineffable Sephiroth have the appearance of the Lightning flash, their origin is unseen and no end is perceived." (Chapter I, §6)

**On the emanation sequence (the ten as Spirit → Elements → Directions):**
> "The ineffable Sephiroth give forth the Ten numbers. First; the Spirit of the God of the living... Second; from the Spirit He produced Air, and formed in it twenty-two sounds-the letters... Third; from the Air He formed the Waters, and from the formless and void made mire and clay... Fourth; from the Water He formed Fire... Fifth [through Tenth]; He sealed the six directions: Height, Depth, East, West, South, North." (Chapter I, §9-11)

**On creation through the letters:**
> "From the non-existent He made Something; and all forms of speech and everything that has been produced; from the empty void He made the material world, and from the inert earth He brought forth everything that hath life." (Chapter II, §6)

## Lurianic Schema (from Scholem 1941, 1974 — scholarly reconstruction)

### Tzimtzum (Contraction)
> "Luria's most crucial and original contribution — the doctrine of Tzimtzum... God was compelled to make room for the world by, as it were, abandoning a region within Himself, a kind of mystical primordial space from which He withdrew in order to return to it in the act of creation." (Scholem 1941, p. 261)

### Adam Kadmon (Primordial Man)
> "Into the primordial space thus formed, God projected a ray of His light and this was the beginning of cosmic history... The first being to emerge from this process was Adam Kadmon, 'Primordial Man', a purely spiritual configuration of the divine light." (Scholem 1941, p. 263-264)

### Shevirat HaKelim (Breaking of the Vessels)
> "The beam of Ein-Sof's light that entered the empty space [via Adam Kadmon] had to organize itself in the form of sefirot... But the vessels proved too weak to contain the pure divine light; they broke and shattered. This Breaking of the Vessels (shevirat ha-kelim)... brought about a fundamental disharmony in all of the worlds." (Fine 2003, p. 126)

### Nitzotzot and Tikkun (Sparks and Restoration)
> "The breaking of the vessels scattered sparks (nitzotzot) of divine light throughout the lower worlds, where they became 'trapped' within material shells (kelipot). The purpose of human existence is the liberation and restoration of these sparks through righteous action (tikkun)." (Scholem 1974, article "Shevirat ha-Kelim")
