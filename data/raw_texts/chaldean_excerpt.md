## Key Oracle Fragments

**On the Father (source):**
> "Containing all things in the one summit of his own Hyparxis, He Himself subsists wholly beyond." (Oracle 7)

> "For nothing imperfect emanates from the Paternal Principle." (Oracle 9)

> "Such is the Mind which is energized before energy, while yet it had not gone forth, but abode in the Paternal Depth, and in the Adytum of God nourished silence." (Oracle 12)

> "The Monad first existed, and the Paternal Monad still subsists." (Oracle 25)

**On the double-mind structure:**
> "All things have issued from that one Fire. The Father perfected all things, and delivered them over to the Second Mind, whom all Nations of Men call the First." (Oracle 13)

> "The Second Mind conducts the Empyrean World." (Oracle 14)

> "Power is with them, but Mind is from Him." (Oracle 16)

**On the Dyad / Hecate / World-Soul:**
> "When the Monad is extended, the Dyad is generated." (Oracle 26)

> "And beside Him is seated the Dyad which glitters with intellectual sections, to govern all things, and to order everything not ordered." (Oracle 27)

> "The Mind of the Father said that all things should be cut into Three, whose Will assented, and immediately all things were so divided." (Oracle 28)

**On the Soul:**
> "After the Paternal Conception I the Soul reside, a heat animating all things. ...For he placed the Intelligible in the Soul, and the Soul in dull body." (Oracle 18)

> "The Soul, being a brilliant Fire, by the power of the Father remaineth immortal, and is Mistress of Life, and filleth up the many recesses of the bosom of the World." (Oracle 20)

**On the triadic principle:**
> "For in each World shineth the Triad, over which the Monad ruleth." (Oracle 36)

> "All things are supplied from the bosom of this Triad." (Oracle 31)

**On the material world:**
> "Stoop not down to the darkly splendid World, For a precipice lieth beneath the Earth, a descent of seven steps, and therein is established the throne of an evil and fatal force." (Introduction, line 489)

> "Exalted upon High and animating Light, Fire, Ether and Worlds." (Oracle 38)

## Structural Commentary (from Introduction)

> "Chaldasan Theology contemplated three great divisions of supra-mundane things:—the First was Eternal, without beginning or end, being the 'Paternal Depth,' the bosom of the Deity. The Second was conceived to be that mode of being having beginning but no end; the Creative World or Empyraesum falls under this head... The third and last order of divine things had a beginning in time and will end, this is the transitory Ethereal World." (lines 309-319)

> "Prior to the supramundane Light lay the 'Paternal Depth,' the Absolute Deity, containing all things 'in potentia' and eternally immanent. This is analogous to the Ain Suph Aur of the Kabalah... the Demiurgus, Hypezokos, or Flower of Fire." (lines 321-375)
