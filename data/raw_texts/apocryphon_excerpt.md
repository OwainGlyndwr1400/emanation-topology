# Apocryphon of John — Key Passages for Emanation-Schema Encoding

**Source:** The Apocryphon of John (II,1), in *The Nag Hammadi Library in English*, ed. James M. Robinson, trans. Frederik Wisse (HarperCollins, 1990)
**PDF:** `Research/the nag hammadi library.pdf` — pages 120-136
**Version:** Long version (Codex II,1). Cross-referenced with IV,1 and BG 8502,2 variants noted in the translation.

Only the cosmogony sections (the emanation cascade from the Invisible Spirit to the material world) are excerpted. Narrative framing, body-part catalogues, and soteriological sections are excluded.

## Emanation Architecture (extracted from PDF pp. 122-127)

### Level 0: The Invisible Spirit (The Monad)

**Source:** PDF pp. 122-123 (NHC II 2,26-4,26)

The Monad is described in an extended negative theology: beyond being, beyond thought, beyond naming. It is the absolute source.

Key attributes from the text: monarchy with nothing above it; exists as God and Father of everything; invisible; incorruption in pure light; more than a god; illimitable; unsearchable; immeasurable; immeasurable light, pure, holy, immaculate; neither corporeal nor incorporeal; neither large nor small; his essence does not partake in the aeons nor in time.

### Level 1: Barbelo (First Thought / Forethought)

**Source:** PDF pp. 123-124 (NHC II 4,26-6,10)

Barbelo emerges as the first emanation from the Invisible Spirit: "his thought performed a deed and she came forth." She is called: the first power, the glory of Barbelo, the perfect glory, the forethought of the All, the Mother-Father, the first man, the holy Spirit, the thrice-male, the thrice-powerful, the androgynous one.

Barbelo then requests and receives from the Invisible Spirit a "pentad of aeons": Foreknowledge, Indestructibility, Eternal Life, and Truth. These are attributes of Barbelo, not separate ontological levels.

### Level 2: Autogenes (The Self-Generated / Christ)

**Source:** PDF pp. 124-125 (NHC II 6,10-8,28)

The Invisible Spirit looks at Barbelo and conceives a "spark of light" — the only-begotten child of the Mother-Father. This is the divine Autogenes, identified with Christ. The Spirit anoints him with goodness and places him "over everything." Autogenes is given Mind (Nous) as a fellow worker.

Autogenes/Christ then creates everything through the Word — "Christ the divine Autogenes created everything."

### Level 3: The Four Luminaries (Light-Aeons)

**Source:** PDF p. 125 (NHC II 8,1-9,5)

From Autogenes/Christ, the Four Luminaries appear: Armozel, Oriel, Daveithai, and Eleleth. Each has three attendant aeons (totaling 12 aeons). These are the structural pillars of the Pleroma.

The Four Luminaries contain:
- Armozel: grace, truth, form
- Oriel: conception, perception, memory
- Daveithai: understanding, love, idea
- Eleleth: perfection, peace, wisdom

### Level 3 (parallel): Adamas (Perfect Man / Pigeradamas)

**Source:** PDF pp. 125-126 (NHC II 8,28-9,14)

Through the Autogenes' will, the perfect Man appears — Pigeradamas (Adamas). He is placed in the first aeon with Armozel. His son Seth is placed in the second aeon with Oriel. The seed of Seth is placed in the third aeon with Daveithai.

### Level 4: Sophia's Error

**Source:** PDF p. 126 (NHC II 9,25-10,19)

"Sophia of the Epinoia, being an aeon, conceived a thought from herself... She wanted to bring forth a likeness out of herself WITHOUT the consent of the Spirit — he had not approved — and WITHOUT her consort."

The result: "something came out of her which was imperfect and different from her appearance, because she had created it without her consort." The offspring has a lion-faced serpent form. She names him Yaltabaoth.

This is the key fragmentation event in the Gnostic cosmos.

### Level 5: Yaltabaoth (The Demiurge / Chief Archon)

**Source:** PDF pp. 126-127 (NHC II 10,19-12,25)

Yaltabaoth "took a great power from his mother" and "removed himself from her." He "created for himself other aeons" and "begot authorities for himself." He creates twelve authorities (named) and then seven kings over the seven heavens.

He is "impious in his arrogance" and declares: "I am God and there is no other God beside me" — "for he is ignorant of his strength, the place from which he had come."

The archons create 365 angels. Seven powers are named with animal faces.

### Level 6: The Material World / Created Humanity

**Source:** PDF pp. 128-133 (NHC II 13-20)

Yaltabaoth and the archons create the material human being in the image of the divine Man (Adamas) whose reflection appeared in the waters. But the material body is inert until Yaltabaoth is tricked into breathing his mother's stolen power/light into it.
